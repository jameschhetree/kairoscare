Claude Agent Prompt: Build KairosCare MVP

You are my senior full-stack product engineer, UX designer, and startup MVP architect.

We are building KairosCare, a B2B SaaS platform for home care agencies. The product is a real-time family visibility and wellness layer for home care. We are not trying to replace HHAeXchange, WellSky, AxisCare, AlayaCare, CareSmartz360, or other back-office home care systems. We are building the layer those tools do not do well: real-time, emotionally reassuring, family-facing care updates powered by CNAs.

The core thesis:

Home care agencies already have software for scheduling, EVV, billing, payroll, and documentation. But families still feel anxious because they do not know what happened during the visit. Agencies lose clients because of poor communication. CNAs are the daily users, but they cannot feel surveilled or burdened. The product must make CNAs’ jobs easier, not harder.

KairosCare should serve four portals:

1. CNA Portal
2. Patient / Family Portal
3. Home Care Agency Portal
4. KairosCare Internal Admin Backend

Important: There is currently no logo, branding, design system, or visual identity. You should propose a clean, modern brand direction and implement a polished MVP UI. The brand should feel trustworthy, warm, healthcare-safe, modern, and family-centered. Think: healthcare SaaS meets Brightwheel/ClassDojo-style family reassurance, but for elder care.


Product Positioning

KairosCare is:

A real-time care visibility platform for home care agencies that lets CNAs quickly log visit moments, while families receive reassuring updates and agencies gain proof of communication quality.

Primary buyer:

Home care agency owners / operators.

Primary daily user:

CNAs / caregivers.

Secondary user:

Adult children, spouses, siblings, and family decision-makers.

Internal user:

KairosCare team managing agencies, users, billing status, compliance, and support.

This is B2B first. Agencies pay. Families are invited for free. CNAs are never charged.


MVP Goal

Build a working MVP that can demo the full loop:

1. Agency creates a client/patient profile.
2. Agency assigns a CNA to that client.
3. CNA logs a visit update from mobile.
4. CNA adds check-in, meal, mood, activity, note, and optional photo.
5. Family receives a timeline update.
6. Family can react or leave a short thank-you.
7. Agency sees engagement metrics and client activity.
8. KairosCare internal admin can manage all agencies and users.

The MVP should feel real enough to pilot with 1–3 home care agencies.

Compliance and Security Requirements

This product touches healthcare-adjacent and likely PHI data, especially photos, mood, meal, and care notes. Build with HIPAA-aware architecture from the start.

For MVP:

- Role-based access control
- Multi-tenant database model
- Agency data isolation
- Encrypted file storage
- Signed photo URLs
- No public media URLs
- Audit logs for PHI access
- No PHI in emails, SMS, or push notifications
- Secure authentication
- Strong password requirements
- MFA-ready for agency/admin users
- Clear consent language
- BAA-ready vendor assumptions

Add compliance disclaimers in the app where needed.

Do not build:

- Full EVV
- Billing
- Payroll
- Medicare documentation
- Clinical diagnosis tools
- AI medical advice
- Independent caregiver marketplace
- Wearable integrations

MVP Acceptance Criteria

The MVP is successful when:

1. I can log in as a CNA and submit a visit update.
2. I can log in as a family member and see that update in a beautiful timeline.
3. I can log in as an agency admin and see clients, CNAs, assignments, and engagement metrics.
4. I can log in as KairosCare admin and manage agencies.
5. Role permissions work.
6. Seed data works.
7. Photo upload is implemented or convincingly stubbed.
8. Notifications are implemented or convincingly stubbed.
9. The UI is polished enough to show to a pilot agency.
10. The codebase is clean, documented, and ready for iteration.

Extra Suggestions

Add Spanish support in v1, at least for CNA flows. The report specifically emphasizes caregiver adoption risk, and a lot of CNA/home care workflows will benefit from bilingual UX.

Make agency approval optional. Some agencies may want real-time updates to families, while others may want staff to review posts first. Build a setting: “Auto-publish CNA updates” on/off.

Avoid family-to-CNA direct messaging in v1 because it can overwhelm CNAs. Safer MVP: families can react, comment, or send a concern to the agency.
